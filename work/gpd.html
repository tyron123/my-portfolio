Graphical Process Designer (GPD)

  • Role: Senior UX Designer
  • Company: TOPdesk GmbH
  • Duration: 10 months (original research) + ongoing MVP prototype 3 months
  • Team: 6 UX designers, 5 front-end developers (then); now 1 PO, 4 developers, 1 tester, 1 designer, 1 scrum master, 1 solutions architect
  • Tools: Figma, Miro, Affinity Mapping, BPMN, HTML5, SVG
  • Status: MVP prototype built and being tested
  • Vision: Design-driven “Design Hub” in ITSM

1. The Call to Adventure

TOPdesk’s ITSM platform was fragmented and siloed. Each request type lived in its own module, leading to clunky UX and weak cross-team collaboration...

2. The Challenge

Org barriers: Stakeholder hesitation; no clear MVP at the start.

Technical constraints: Legacy monolith; move to HTML5/SVG canvas.

UX hurdles: Accessible drag‑and‑drop; simplifying gateways/logic.

3. The Journey

Phase 1 (4 years ago): Affinity diagrams, workflow maps, personas, UX audits; 30+ insights (clicks, terminology, keyboard needs, automation expectations).

Phase 2 (today): Lo‑fi to hi‑fi MVP; canvas tool, toolbar, side panel; command bar; keyboard-first interactions; SVG/DOM links; testing with real workflows.

4. The Transformation

MVP highlights: BPMN‑style design; manual/system tasks; OOTB workflows; API-integrated request card.

Solves: Replaces siloed modules; simplifies routing/visibility; modern interaction model; groundwork for automation/analytics/AI.

Proud of: Deep accessibility strategy; new interaction paradigm; building on years of research.

5. The Return

Learned: Cross-team collaboration is mandatory; side‑panel task behavior was hardest and most impactful; alignment equals progress.

Trade‑offs: No multi‑language; no AI at launch; limited BPMN set.

Next (Design Hub): Form Designer, Workflow Designer, Action/Event Manager, Service Designer, SSP Designer. AI assists, analytics, NLP workflows, intelligent validation.

Crafting seamless, empowering experiences that connect people, purpose, and technology.
